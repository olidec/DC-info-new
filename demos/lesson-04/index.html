Marie Curie

Physicist and chemist, 1867–1934

Biography

Marie Curie was born in Warsaw in 1867. She moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne, where she later became the first woman to earn a doctorate in physics in France.

Together with her husband Pierre, she discovered two new elements: polonium and radium.

Nobel Prizes

  1. Physics, 1903 — shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel
  2. Chemistry, 1911 — for her discovery of radium and polonium

Key Facts

  • Born: 7 November 1867, Warsaw, Poland
  • Died: 4 July 1934, Passy, France
  • Field: Physics, Chemistry
